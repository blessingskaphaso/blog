THE MULTIFACETED REALITY OF SUCCESS

Today we are living in an era and a world or a society where the word "success" has become a household name. Every one of us desires to win, progress and succeed. Sometimes even at any cost.

We see that many people are hungry for success today. Thus hunger sometimes leads to an increasing competition among people. There are also times when this kind of competition becomes very unhealthy. This usually happens because along the way, we sometimes lose the meaning and essence of success.

It would be of necessary importance for us to go back to the drawing board and remind ourselves of what success really entails. Combing through its etymological roots, the word success is derived from the Latin noun successus that comes from the Latin verb succedere which means "to come after". As such success can be said to be that which comes after. Success is usually a result of some hard work or effort. It is otherwise defined as the accomplishment of one's goals. It can be the attainment of wealth, position, honours and so forth.

In this sense, it is very obvious that success can be gauged and viewed differently by various people. For some, success can mean the attainment of happiness and peace of mind in their lives. Others consider success as completion of their academic endeavors. Some people also see success as outsmarting others. For some, success entails living a lavish life, having a lot of money, material goods, living a comfortable life, having freedom and independence while for some success lies in having profound self-knowledge. Most importantly, I feel that success should be a positive element in it.

Over and above all, we have seen that the surface area for success is too large to be covered by a single individual. As such no one should be pressurized in life to reach the heights of what others call success. We ought not to be intimidated by others people's success. Objectively, success should entail some progress in life no matter how small it might look.

We can all succeed even in different fields and uplift each other. We just need to keep pushing and doing the most with all the things before us and at our disposal. We will gradually get where we have so long desired to be.

Traps within Growth

Have you ever imagined a life without worries, problems or fears? A life with all that one desires? Have you ever thought of a life in which wishes immediately come into existence?...

Have you ever imagined a life without worries, problems or fears? A life with all that one desires? Have you ever thought of a life in which wishes immediately come into existence? The truth is such a life has never existed before, does not exist and will never exist. So we have two choices at our disposal, either to fight and embrace the life snags and live or die.

'Everyone looks happy until you talk to them'. This expression had been useless to me until time revealed the richness within it (the expression). As growth continues to dictate the rules of nature, I once heard one old man saying, 'Never grow old, it's a trap.' If I may be honest, I hated him because I felt threatened. By the way, pardon me, I was just young and my mind was not yet prepared to digest deeper truths.

As we grow, we are sometimes knocked down, tired, frustrated, devastated and sometimes bored with our own being. We encounter snags; insults, unfulfilled wishes, heartbreaks, betrays from friends and all sorts of hardships. If you have never met one, wait or thank God. Sometimes we wish we were someone, more handsome or more beautiful. We wish we had more than what we have now, more money, more friends, more everything. We wish we were just a different person. Unfortunately, many turn away from the truth and live a life hypocrisy, pretending everything is well. But nature brings us back to where we belong and remind us what we are.

Growth is a series of confusion, a war that only favours the strong. It is a tag of war between the self and society, a conflict between needs and wants, a combat between the present self and the future ideal self, a clash between the self and peers. It is a confusion because much as the above edify one's growth on one hand, they are potential obstacles on the other hand. The confusion is everyone's as far as he or she is developing.

Unfortunately, we find ourselves existing in this world of confusion, the world we never took part in designing yet we wish it to operate in accordance with our ambitions. So what do we do, do we give up and die or embrace it and live? We have to dance to some tunes, regret some, learn and move on. Nothing in nature blossoms all the year around. As far as we are part of nature, we cannot flourish all the time.

In the words of the German-American psychoanalyst, Erick Erickson, there comes a moment as we grow old when we either enjoy integrity or suffer despair. The former makes one proud of the life well lived. The latter haunts one for the life wasted. Unfortunately, when we grow old, there is no time to go back and change things. The nights of the chances missed never die. So, what doing currently, preparing for old days of anguish or integrity?

An exploration of Jesus' profound message in the Beatitudes and how these ancient teachings can transform our modern daily lives.
